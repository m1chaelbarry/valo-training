    RAMP Protocol

    A 10–15 min warmup designed to prime your CNS — not tire it out. Stop before fatigue.

    Critical rule: Do NOT warm up for longer than 15 minutes before ranked. Overwarming causes premature CNS fatigue and degrades your first-bullet accuracy. Finish fresh, then queue immediately.
    R
    RAISE 2–3 min

    Elevate heart rate and blood flow. Wake up your hands. No pressure on performance.

    • Practice range — loose, stress-free shooting at bots
    • Buy a Vandal or Phantom — doesn't matter which
    • Shoot medium bots at head height, left to right
    • No score tracking, no frustration allowed
    • You are waking up your nervous system, not practicing
    2:30
    A
    ACTIVATE 2–3 min

    Wake up precise crosshair control. Calm, stable tracking to stabilize your hand.

    • Switch to Ghost or Sheriff
    • Slow micro-adjustments onto bot heads — deliberate, not rushed
    • Miyagi method: track a bot head without shooting first
    • Focus: idle crosshair during strafe, single small correction after stop
    • If hand feels shaky, slow down — don't force speed here
    2:30
    M
    MOBILIZE 2–3 min

    Open your full arm range of motion. Ensure no part of the pad is unreachable.

    • Wide swipes across the full mousepad — left edge to right edge
    • Distant flicks to targets at the far end of the range
    • Practice 180° turns — make sure your arm isn't restricted
    • Fizzle tension at flick end — explosive start, completely loose finish
    • This unlocks your shoulder and forearm for match speed
    2:30
    P
    POTENTIATE 5–7 min

    The most important and most neglected phase. Simulate match pace — not max effort.

    • One focused DM at tournament pace
    • Pick ONE mechanical cue only (e.g. "idle crosshair" or "two steps then stop")
    • Do NOT try to win the DM — ignore your score
    • Do NOT add more drills if you "don't feel ready" — you are wasting CNS budget
    • Finish this phase fresh and slightly elevated — not tired
    • Queue ranked immediately after — the window is ~10 minutes

    DM weapon for Potentiate:

    Ghost — placement & patience Guardian — stop timing & one-tap Sheriff — composure under pressure
    6:00

    After RAMP — Queue Immediately

    The CNS potentiation effect lasts approximately 10 minutes. Do not scroll your phone, do not watch a video. Queue the second you finish your Potentiate DM. Every minute you wait degrades the warmup effect.

    Off-PC Protocol

    After game, no computer. This is your highest-ROI recovery.

    1
    Idle Rest — 5–10 min

    Close your eyes. No phone, no podcast, no new input. Your hippocampus replays successful motor sequences in reverse — this accelerates retention by 20–30%. This is free skill consolidation.

    2
    Mental Visualization — 10–15 min

    Replay scenarios from today's session. Imagine the correct peek, the exact two-step stop, the breath before swinging. Visualization activates the same upper motor neurons as physical practice — up to 35% improvement with 15 min/day.

    3
    Sleep — prioritize this

    Motor skills are consolidated in NREM2 sleep (sleep spindles). Players who sleep well after training sessions show 20–30% better accuracy and speed the next day. No sleep = wasted session. This is not optional.
